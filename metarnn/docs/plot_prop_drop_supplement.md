# Proportion-Drop Supplement Figure

**Script:** `metarnn/lib/plot_prop_drop_supplement.py`

## Purpose

Shows how the NN's gaze-based choice prediction accuracy (cross-validated logistic regression) degrades as fixations are randomly dropped. This demonstrates that NN performance with recall-calibrated fixation loss is comparable to human performance, linking the NN's full-fixation advantage to the information loss from imperfect episodic recall.

## Input Data

1. **Drop-sweep summary CSV** (`drop_sweep_summary.csv`): Consolidated results from running `analysis/lib/predict_choice_from_item_prop_time_interactions.py` at 10 drop fractions (0%, 10%, ..., 90%). Generated by the drop-sweep step in `metarnn/create_nn_figures.sh`. Columns: `drop_pct`, `retained_frac`, `cv_mean`, `cv_sem`.

2. **Human CV accuracy summary** (`output/eyegaze/stats/summary_prop_time_location_interactions_recalled_all_norm-within.csv`): Human choice prediction accuracy using recalled reward values, for horizontal reference.

3. **Recall proportion CSV** (`output/eyegaze/recall/recall_sig_timepoint_prop_fix_time.csv`): Mean proportion of fixation time at recall-significant timepoints, for vertical reference. Computed by `analysis/lib/compute_recall_drop_fraction.py`.

## Methods

- At each drop fraction, a random subset of individual fixation rows is removed before recomputing fixation-time aggregates and fitting the 10-fold cross-validated L2-regularized logistic regression model predicting take/leave choice from proportion-time x location x reward interactions.
- The curve shows mean CV accuracy +/- 95% CI (1.96 x SEM) across folds.
- The human horizontal band shows human CV accuracy +/- 95% CI for comparison.
- The vertical recall-proportion lines mark the mean (+/- 95% CI) proportion of fixation time devoted to items at timepoints where recall had not yet significantly decayed, providing a principled estimate of how much fixation information is available under realistic memory constraints.
